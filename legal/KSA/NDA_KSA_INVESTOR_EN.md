## RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Disclosing Party is engaged in the development and operation of a financial technology platform providing financial analysis, valuation, and intelligence services;

WHEREAS, the Receiving Party is interested in evaluating a potential investment in the Disclosing Party;

WHEREAS, in connection with such evaluation, the Disclosing Party may disclose certain confidential and proprietary information to the Receiving Party;

WHEREAS, the Disclosing Party desires to protect the confidentiality of such information;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements contained herein, and in compliance with the laws and regulations of the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, the Parties agree as follows:

---

## 1. DEFINITIONS

### 1.1 "Confidential Information"

"Confidential Information" means any and all information disclosed by the Disclosing Party to the Receiving Party in connection with the Purpose, whether orally, in writing, electronically, or by any other means, whether or not marked as confidential, including but not limited to:

**(a) Business Information:**
- Business plans, strategies, and operational information
- Financial statements, projections, budgets, and forecasts
- Funding history, capitalization tables, and equity structures
- Revenue figures, profit margins, and expense details
- Customer and client lists, demographics, and retention data
- Vendor and supplier relationships and terms
- Market research, competitive analyses, and market positioning

**(b) Technical Information:**
- Technology overview and system architecture
- Product roadmaps and development plans
- Intellectual property portfolio and patent applications
- Proprietary algorithms and methodologies
- Key technical differentiators and competitive advantages

**(c) Operational Information:**
- Key personnel information and organizational structure
- Material contracts and partnership agreements
- Regulatory filings and compliance status
- Pending or threatened litigation

**(d) Strategic Information:**
- Expansion plans and market entry strategies (including Saudi and international markets)
- Merger, acquisition, or partnership discussions
- Pricing strategies and competitive positioning
- Any information designated as "Confidential" or "سري" at the time of disclosure

### 1.2 "Purpose"

"Purpose" means the Receiving Party's evaluation of a potential investment in, acquisition of, or other business transaction with the Disclosing Party.

### 1.3 "Representatives"

"Representatives" means the Receiving Party's officers, directors, employees, partners, members, agents, attorneys, accountants, financial advisors, and other professional advisors who have a need to know the Confidential Information for the Purpose.

---

## 2. EXCLUSIONS FROM CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:

**(a)** Was publicly known and generally available prior to disclosure;

**(b)** Becomes publicly known through no wrongful act or omission of the Receiving Party or its Representatives;

**(c)** Was in the Receiving Party's lawful possession prior to disclosure, as evidenced by written records;

**(d)** Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of the Confidential Information;

**(e)** Is lawfully obtained from a third party without restriction or breach of confidentiality;

**(f)** Is required to be disclosed by law, court order, or governmental authority in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, provided that the Receiving Party gives prompt notice to the Disclosing Party (to the extent legally permitted) and cooperates in seeking a protective order.

---

## 3. OBLIGATIONS OF RECEIVING PARTY

### 3.1 Confidentiality Obligations

The Receiving Party agrees to:

**(a)** Hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence;

**(b)** Not disclose any Confidential Information to any third party without prior written consent;

**(c)** Use the Confidential Information solely for the Purpose;

**(d)** Limit access to Representatives who have a need to know and are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as restrictive as this Agreement;

**(e)** Be responsible for any breach by its Representatives;

**(f)** Not copy or reproduce Confidential Information except as reasonably necessary for the Purpose;

**(g)** Promptly notify the Disclosing Party of any unauthorized disclosure or use.

### 3.2 Investment-Specific Restrictions

The Receiving Party shall NOT:

**(a)** Use Confidential Information to make investment decisions in any competing company;

**(b)** Share Confidential Information with any portfolio company that competes with the Disclosing Party;

**(c)** Use Confidential Information to assist any third party in developing competing products or services;

**(d)** Use Confidential Information for any purpose that violates the laws or regulations of the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia or the Capital Market Authority (CMA).

---

## 4. NO OBLIGATION TO PROCEED

### 4.1 No Binding Commitment

This Agreement does not obligate either Party to:

**(a)** Enter into any investment, acquisition, or other business transaction;

**(b)** Continue discussions or negotiations beyond any point;

**(c)** Disclose any particular Confidential Information;

**(d)** Refrain from entering into transactions with third parties.

### 4.2 No Representations or Warranties

The Disclosing Party makes no representations or warranties, express or implied, regarding the accuracy, completeness, or fitness for any purpose of the Confidential Information. The Receiving Party acknowledges that it shall rely solely on its own investigation and due diligence.

### 4.3 No Exclusivity

This Agreement does not grant the Receiving Party any exclusive right to evaluate or invest in the Disclosing Party.

---

## 5. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

### 5.1 No License Granted

Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed as granting the Receiving Party any right, title, license, or interest in or to the Confidential Information or any intellectual property of the Disclosing Party.

### 5.2 Ownership

All Confidential Information remains the sole and exclusive property of the Disclosing Party.

---

## 6. TERM AND TERMINATION

### 6.1 Agreement Term

This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in effect until terminated by either Party upon fifteen (15) days' prior written notice, or upon conclusion of discussions regarding the Purpose, whichever occurs first.

### 6.2 Survival of Obligations

The obligations of confidentiality shall survive termination as follows:

**(a)** For trade secrets: Indefinitely, or for so long as such information qualifies as a trade secret;

**(b)** For all other Confidential Information: Five (5) years from the date of disclosure.

---

## 7. RETURN OR DESTRUCTION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

### 7.1 Return or Destruction

Upon termination of this Agreement, or upon written request by the Disclosing Party, the Receiving Party shall, within thirty (30) days:

**(a)** Return all tangible materials containing Confidential Information;

**(b)** Permanently delete all electronic copies of Confidential Information;

**(c)** Destroy all notes, analyses, or materials derived from Confidential Information.

### 7.2 Certification

Upon request, the Receiving Party shall provide written certification of compliance with this Section.

### 7.3 Retained Copies

The Receiving Party may retain copies solely as required by law or internal compliance policies, provided such copies remain subject to confidentiality obligations.

---

## 8. NON-SOLICITATION

### 8.1 Non-Solicitation of Employees

During the term of this Agreement and for a period of two (2) years thereafter, the Receiving Party shall not directly solicit for employment any key employee of the Disclosing Party with whom the Receiving Party had contact during the evaluation process, without the Disclosing Party's prior written consent.

---

## 9. REMEDIES

### 9.1 Irreparable Harm

The Receiving Party acknowledges that:

**(a)** The Confidential Information is valuable and unique;

**(b)** Unauthorized disclosure will cause irreparable harm;

**(c)** The Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief and other equitable remedies available under the laws of the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, in addition to any other remedies at law.

### 9.2 Indemnification

The Receiving Party shall indemnify and hold harmless the Disclosing Party from any claims, damages, losses, and expenses arising from any breach of this Agreement.

---

## 10. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

### 10.1 Amicable Settlement

The Parties shall first attempt to resolve any dispute through good faith negotiations for a period of thirty (30) days.

### 10.2 Arbitration

If the dispute cannot be resolved through negotiation, it shall be settled by binding arbitration administered by the Saudi Center for Commercial Arbitration ("SCCA") under its Arbitration Rules.

### 10.3 Arbitration Procedures

**(a)** One (1) arbitrator selected per SCCA rules;

**(b)** Place of arbitration: Riyadh, Kingdom of Saudi Arabia;

**(c)** Language: Arabic (with English translation as needed);

**(d)** Decision final and binding;

**(e)** Costs shared equally; each Party bears its own legal fees.

### 10.4 Provisional Remedies

Either Party may seek provisional or injunctive relief from competent Saudi courts pending arbitration.
