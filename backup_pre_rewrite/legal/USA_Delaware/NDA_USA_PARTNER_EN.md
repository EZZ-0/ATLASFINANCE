## RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Parties wish to explore a potential business relationship, including but not limited to data partnerships, API integrations, technology collaborations, or other commercial arrangements (the "Purpose");

WHEREAS, in connection with such discussions, each Party may disclose certain confidential and proprietary information to the other Party;

WHEREAS, the Parties desire to protect the confidentiality of their respective Confidential Information;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements contained herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties agree as follows:

---

## 1. DEFINITIONS

### 1.1 "Confidential Information"

"Confidential Information" means any and all information disclosed by either Party (the "Disclosing Party") to the other Party (the "Receiving Party"), whether orally, in writing, electronically, or by any other means, whether or not marked as confidential, including but not limited to:

**(a) Technical Information:**
- Source code, object code, APIs, and software documentation
- System architecture, database schemas, and technical specifications
- Algorithms, formulas, and proprietary methodologies
- Product roadmaps and development plans
- Security protocols and authentication mechanisms
- API keys, credentials, and access tokens

**(b) Business Information:**
- Business plans, strategies, and operational information
- Financial statements, pricing models, and cost structures
- Customer and client lists, usage data, and analytics
- Marketing strategies and competitive analyses
- Vendor relationships and terms

**(c) Integration-Specific Information:**
- Data formats, schemas, and transformation rules
- Integration architectures and data flows
- Testing credentials and sandbox environments
- Performance benchmarks and SLAs
- Error handling and logging specifications

**(d) Other Information:**
- Any information designated as "Confidential" or "Proprietary" at disclosure
- Any information that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given its nature and circumstances of disclosure

### 1.2 "Purpose"

"Purpose" means the evaluation, development, and potential implementation of a business partnership, data integration, API connection, or other commercial relationship between the Parties.

### 1.3 "Representatives"

"Representatives" means a Party's officers, directors, employees, agents, contractors, advisors, and any other persons who have a need to know the Confidential Information for the Purpose.

---

## 2. EXCLUSIONS FROM CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:

**(a)** Was publicly known prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party;

**(b)** Becomes publicly known through no fault of the Receiving Party;

**(c)** Was in the Receiving Party's lawful possession prior to disclosure;

**(d)** Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information;

**(e)** Is lawfully obtained from a third party without restriction;

**(f)** Is required to be disclosed by law, provided the Receiving Party gives prompt notice and cooperates in seeking a protective order.

---

## 3. MUTUAL OBLIGATIONS

### 3.1 Confidentiality Obligations

Each Party, as a Receiving Party, agrees to:

**(a)** Hold the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information in strict confidence;

**(b)** Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without prior written consent;

**(c)** Use Confidential Information solely for the Purpose;

**(d)** Limit access to Representatives with a need to know who are bound by comparable confidentiality obligations;

**(e)** Apply at least the same degree of care as it uses to protect its own confidential information, but no less than reasonable care;

**(f)** Promptly notify the Disclosing Party of any unauthorized disclosure or use.

### 3.2 Security Measures

Each Party shall implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect the other Party's Confidential Information, including:

**(a)** Encryption of data in transit and at rest;

**(b)** Access controls and authentication mechanisms;

**(c)** Secure storage and transmission protocols;

**(d)** Regular security assessments and audits;

**(e)** Incident response procedures.

---

## 4. API AND INTEGRATION-SPECIFIC PROVISIONS

### 4.1 API Credentials

**(a)** Each Party shall treat any API keys, tokens, credentials, or access codes provided by the other Party as Confidential Information;

**(b)** API credentials shall only be used for authorized purposes within the scope of the Purpose;

**(c)** Each Party shall immediately notify the other Party if any API credentials are compromised or suspected of compromise;

**(d)** Upon termination, each Party shall immediately revoke and destroy all API credentials provided by the other Party.

### 4.2 Data Handling

**(a)** Each Party shall only access, collect, or process data from the other Party's systems as expressly authorized;

**(b)** Neither Party shall scrape, crawl, or use automated means to extract data beyond authorized API calls;

**(c)** Each Party shall comply with all applicable data protection laws in handling data received from the other Party;

**(d)** Neither Party shall sell, license, or commercially exploit the other Party's data without express written consent.

### 4.3 Rate Limits and Fair Use

Each Party shall respect any rate limits, usage quotas, or fair use policies communicated by the other Party regarding API access or data usage.

---

## 5. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

### 5.1 No License Granted

Nothing in this Agreement grants either Party any right, title, license, or interest in or to the other Party's Confidential Information or intellectual property, except the limited right to use Confidential Information for the Purpose.

### 5.2 Ownership

Each Party retains all right, title, and interest in and to its own Confidential Information and intellectual property.

### 5.3 Joint Development

If the Parties engage in joint development activities, ownership of any resulting intellectual property shall be governed by a separate written agreement.

### 5.4 Feedback

If either Party provides feedback, suggestions, or improvements regarding the other Party's products or services, the receiving Party shall own all rights to such feedback without obligation to the providing Party.

---

## 6. NO OBLIGATION TO PROCEED

### 6.1 No Binding Commitment

This Agreement does not obligate either Party to:

**(a)** Enter into any partnership, integration, or business transaction;

**(b)** Continue discussions or negotiations;

**(c)** Disclose any particular Confidential Information;

**(d)** Grant any license or access rights beyond this Agreement.

### 6.2 No Representations or Warranties

Neither Party makes any representations or warranties regarding the accuracy, completeness, or fitness for purpose of its Confidential Information.

### 6.3 No Exclusivity

This Agreement does not grant either Party exclusive rights to partner with or integrate with the other Party.

---

## 7. TERM AND TERMINATION

### 7.1 Agreement Term

This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in effect for a period of two (2) years, unless earlier terminated by either Party upon thirty (30) days' prior written notice.

### 7.2 Survival of Obligations

The obligations of confidentiality shall survive termination as follows:

**(a)** For trade secrets: Indefinitely, or for so long as such information qualifies as a trade secret;

**(b)** For all other Confidential Information: Five (5) years from the date of disclosure.

---

## 8. RETURN OR DESTRUCTION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

### 8.1 Return or Destruction

Upon termination, or upon written request by either Party, the Receiving Party shall, within thirty (30) days:

**(a)** Return all materials containing the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information;

**(b)** Permanently delete all electronic copies;

**(c)** Revoke and destroy all API credentials and access tokens;

**(d)** Destroy all derived materials.

### 8.2 Certification

Upon request, each Party shall provide written certification of compliance.

### 8.3 Retained Copies

A Party may retain copies as required by law, provided such copies remain subject to confidentiality obligations.

---

## 9. NON-SOLICITATION

### 9.1 Non-Solicitation of Employees

During the term of this Agreement and for a period of two (2) years thereafter, neither Party shall directly solicit for employment any employee of the other Party with whom it had material contact during the Purpose, without prior written consent.

---

## 10. REMEDIES

### 10.1 Irreparable Harm

Each Party acknowledges that:

**(a)** The other Party's Confidential Information is valuable and unique;

**(b)** Unauthorized disclosure will cause irreparable harm;

**(c)** The injured Party shall be entitled to injunctive relief and other equitable remedies, in addition to any remedies at law.

### 10.2 Indemnification

Each Party shall indemnify and hold harmless the other Party from any claims, damages, losses, and expenses arising from any breach of this Agreement.

---

## 11. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

### 11.1 Arbitration

Any dispute arising out of this Agreement shall be settled by binding arbitration administered by the American Arbitration Association ("AAA") under its Commercial Arbitration Rules.

### 11.2 Arbitration Procedures

**(a)** One (1) arbitrator selected per AAA rules;

**(b)** Place of arbitration: Wilmington, Delaware;

**(c)** Language: English;

**(d)** Decision final and binding;

**(e)** Costs and fees shared equally.

### 11.3 Provisional Remedies

Either Party may seek provisional relief from courts pending arbitration.
