## RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Disclosing Party possesses certain confidential and proprietary information relating to its financial technology platform, software, algorithms, business operations, and related intellectual property;

WHEREAS, the Receiving Party will be engaged to provide services to the Disclosing Party and, in connection therewith, may receive or have access to Confidential Information;

## 1. DEFINITIONS

### 1.1 "Confidential Information"

"Confidential Information" means any and all information disclosed by the Disclosing Party to the Receiving Party, whether orally, in writing, electronically, or by any other means, whether or not marked as confidential, including but not limited to:

**(a) Technical Information:**
- Source code, object code, algorithms, and software designs
- Financial modeling algorithms, including Discounted Cash Flow (DCF), Monte Carlo simulation, Weighted Average Cost of Capital (WACC), and valuation methodologies
- Data extraction and processing techniques for SEC EDGAR filings and other financial data sources
- Proprietary validation engines and accuracy verification methods
- System architecture, APIs, databases, and database schemas
- Machine learning models, training data, and analytical frameworks
- User interface designs, user experience flows, and interaction patterns
- Documentation, specifications, and technical drawings

**(b) Business Information:**
- Business plans, strategies, and financial projections
- Pricing models, cost structures, and revenue data
- Marketing strategies and customer acquisition methods
- Customer and client lists, contact information, and preferences
- Vendor and supplier information and relationships
- Market research and competitive analyses

**(c) Financial Information:**
- Financial statements, budgets, and forecasts
- Funding documents, investment terms, and valuation analyses
- Revenue figures, profit margins, and expense details

**(d) Trade Secrets:**
- Proprietary methodologies and processes
- Formulas, patterns, compilations, and devices
- Any information that derives independent economic value from not being generally known

**(e) Other Information:**
- Employee and contractor information
- Research and development activities
- Any information designated as "Confidential," "Proprietary," or similar designation at the time of disclosure or within thirty (30) days thereafter

### 1.2 "Purpose"

"Purpose" means the Receiving Party's engagement to provide development, consulting, design, or other professional services for the Disclosing Party's financial technology platform and related projects.

### 1.3 "Representatives"

"Representatives" means the Receiving Party's employees, agents, contractors, advisors, and any other persons who have a need to know the Confidential Information for the Purpose.

---

## 2. EXCLUSIONS FROM CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:

**(a)** Was publicly known and generally available in the public domain prior to the time of disclosure by the Disclosing Party;

**(b)** Becomes publicly known and generally available through no wrongful act or omission of the Receiving Party or its Representatives;

**(c)** Was in the Receiving Party's lawful possession prior to the disclosure, as evidenced by written records predating such disclosure;

**(d)** Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Confidential Information, as evidenced by written records;

**(e)** Is lawfully obtained by the Receiving Party from a third party who has the right to disclose such information without restriction and without breach of any obligation of confidentiality;

**(f)** Is required to be disclosed by law, court order, or governmental authority, provided that the Receiving Party: (i) gives the Disclosing Party prompt written notice of such requirement (to the extent legally permitted); (ii) cooperates with the Disclosing Party's efforts to obtain a protective order or other appropriate remedy; and (iii) discloses only that portion of the Confidential Information that is legally required to be disclosed.

---

## 3. OBLIGATIONS OF RECEIVING PARTY

### 3.1 Confidentiality Obligations

The Receiving Party agrees to:

**(a)** Hold and maintain all Confidential Information in strict confidence using at least the same degree of care as it uses to protect its own confidential information, but in no event less than reasonable care;

**(b)** Not disclose any Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party;

**(c)** Use the Confidential Information solely for the Purpose and for no other purpose whatsoever;

**(d)** Limit access to Confidential Information to its Representatives who: (i) have a need to know such information for the Purpose; and (ii) are bound by written confidentiality obligations at least as restrictive as those contained herein;

**(e)** Be responsible for any breach of this Agreement by its Representatives;

**(f)** Not copy, reproduce, or duplicate any Confidential Information except as reasonably necessary for the Purpose;

**(g)** Promptly notify the Disclosing Party of any unauthorized disclosure, access, or use of Confidential Information of which the Receiving Party becomes aware.

### 3.2 Security Measures

The Receiving Party shall implement and maintain appropriate administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect Confidential Information, including:

**(a)** Secure storage of physical documents containing Confidential Information;

**(b)** Password protection and encryption for electronic files containing Confidential Information;

**(c)** Secure deletion of Confidential Information when no longer needed for the Purpose;

**(d)** Access controls limiting Confidential Information to authorized personnel only.

### 3.3 Prohibited Activities

The Receiving Party shall NOT:

**(a)** Reverse engineer, disassemble, decompile, or otherwise attempt to derive the source code, structure, or algorithms of any software, technology, or systems included in the Confidential Information;

**(b)** Create derivative works based on the Confidential Information without express written consent;

**(c)** Use Confidential Information to develop, market, or sell any competing product or service;

**(d)** Remove or alter any proprietary notices, labels, or marks on any Confidential Information.

## 4. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY AND WORK PRODUCT

### 4.1 No License Granted

Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed as granting the Receiving Party any right, title, license, or interest in or to the Confidential Information, or any intellectual property rights of the Disclosing Party. All Confidential Information remains the sole and exclusive property of the Disclosing Party.

### 4.2 Work for Hire

Any and all work product, inventions, discoveries, improvements, designs, code, documentation, or other materials created, conceived, or developed by the Receiving Party in connection with the Purpose ("Work Product"), whether or not incorporating Confidential Information, shall be considered "work made for hire" as defined under the United States Copyright Act and shall be the sole and exclusive property of the Disclosing Party.

### 4.3 Assignment of Rights

To the extent any Work Product does not qualify as "work made for hire," the Receiving Party hereby irrevocably assigns to the Disclosing Party all right, title, and interest in and to such Work Product, including all intellectual property rights therein. The Receiving Party agrees to execute any documents and take any actions reasonably requested by the Disclosing Party to evidence, perfect, or enforce such assignment.

### 4.4 Moral Rights Waiver

To the extent permitted by applicable law, the Receiving Party waives any moral rights or similar rights in the Work Product.

### 4.5 Residuals Clause

Nothing in this Agreement shall restrict the Receiving Party from using general knowledge, skills, and experience retained in unaided human memory, provided that such use does not result in the disclosure of specific Confidential Information or the reproduction of Work Product.

## 5. TERM AND TERMINATION

### 5.1 Agreement Term

This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in effect until terminated by either Party upon thirty (30) days' prior written notice to the other Party, or upon completion of the Purpose, whichever occurs first.

### 5.2 Survival of Obligations

The obligations of confidentiality set forth in this Agreement shall survive termination or expiration of this Agreement as follows:

**(a)** For Trade Secrets: Indefinitely, or for so long as such information qualifies as a trade secret under applicable law;

**(b)** For all other Confidential Information: Five (5) years from the date of disclosure.

---

## 6. RETURN OR DESTRUCTION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

### 6.1 Return or Destruction

Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, or upon written request by the Disclosing Party at any time, the Receiving Party shall, within thirty (30) days:

**(a)** Promptly return to the Disclosing Party all documents, materials, and other tangible items containing or embodying Confidential Information;

**(b)** Permanently delete or destroy all electronic copies of Confidential Information from all systems, storage media, and devices;

**(c)** Destroy all notes, summaries, analyses, or other materials prepared by the Receiving Party that contain or are derived from Confidential Information.

### 6.2 Certification

Upon request, the Receiving Party shall provide the Disclosing Party with written certification, signed by an authorized representative, confirming that all Confidential Information has been returned, deleted, or destroyed in accordance with this Section 6.

### 6.3 Retained Copies

Notwithstanding the foregoing, the Receiving Party may retain copies of Confidential Information solely to the extent: (a) required by applicable law or professional standards; or (b) automatically retained in routine backup systems, provided that such retained copies remain subject to the confidentiality obligations of this Agreement.

## 7. NON-SOLICITATION

### 7.1 Non-Solicitation of Employees

During the term of this Agreement and for a period of two (2) years thereafter, the Receiving Party shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit, recruit, hire, or attempt to solicit, recruit, or hire any employee or contractor of the Disclosing Party with whom the Receiving Party had contact or about whom the Receiving Party received Confidential Information during the term of this Agreement.

### 7.2 Non-Solicitation of Clients

During the term of this Agreement and for a period of two (2) years thereafter, the Receiving Party shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit, contact, or attempt to divert any client, customer, or business relationship of the Disclosing Party for the purpose of providing services that are competitive with those of the Disclosing Party.

## 8. REMEDIES

### 8.1 Irreparable Harm

The Receiving Party acknowledges and agrees that:

**(a)** The Confidential Information is valuable and unique;

**(b)** Any unauthorized disclosure, use, or breach of this Agreement will cause irreparable harm to the Disclosing Party for which monetary damages alone would be inadequate;

**(c)** The Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief, specific performance, and any other equitable remedies, without the necessity of proving actual damages or posting a bond, in addition to any other remedies available at law or in equity.

### 8.2 Indemnification

The Receiving Party shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the Disclosing Party and its officers, directors, employees, agents, and affiliates from and against any and all claims, damages, losses, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of or relating to any breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party or its Representatives.

### 8.3 Cumulative Remedies

The rights and remedies provided herein are cumulative and are in addition to any other rights and remedies available at law or in equity.

## 9. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

### 9.1 Arbitration

Any dispute, controversy, or claim arising out of or relating to this Agreement, or the breach, termination, or validity thereof, shall be finally settled by binding arbitration administered by the American Arbitration Association ("AAA") in accordance with its Commercial Arbitration Rules.

### 9.2 Arbitration Procedures

**(a)** The arbitration shall be conducted by one (1) arbitrator selected in accordance with AAA rules;

**(b)** The place of arbitration shall be Wilmington, Delaware, United States;

**(c)** The language of the arbitration shall be English;

**(d)** The arbitrator's decision shall be final and binding, and judgment upon the award may be entered in any court of competent jurisdiction;

**(e)** Each Party shall bear its own costs and expenses, and the Parties shall share equally the fees and expenses of the arbitrator.

### 9.3 Provisional Remedies

Notwithstanding the foregoing, either Party may seek provisional or injunctive relief from any court of competent jurisdiction pending the outcome of arbitration, to the extent necessary to protect its Confidential Information or intellectual property rights.
